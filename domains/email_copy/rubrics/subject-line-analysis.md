# Rubric: Subject Line Analysis

Evaluation framework for email subject lines.

## Scoring

### 5 - Exceptional
- Creates genuine curiosity or urgency
- Specific and relevant to recipient
- Would stand out in a crowded inbox
- Promises value that the email delivers
- Appropriate length for mobile preview

### 4 - Strong
- Clear value or curiosity hook
- Relevant to the audience
- Not obviously promotional
- Minor improvements possible

### 3 - Adequate
- Understandable purpose
- Generic but not offensive
- Would get some opens
- Missing specificity or hook

### 2 - Weak
- Vague or boring
- Obviously promotional
- No curiosity or urgency
- Easily ignored

### 1 - Slop
- ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation
- Spam trigger words
- Misleading or clickbait
- Completely generic

## Red Flags (Instant Slop Indicators)
- ALL CAPS words
- Multiple exclamation marks!!!
- "Free" or "Act Now" (spam triggers)
- Emojis overload
- [FIRST_NAME] without context
- "Newsletter" or "Update" alone
- Clickbait that email doesn't deliver on

## Evaluation Questions
1. Would I open this in my own inbox?
2. Does it pass the "scroll test" on mobile?
3. Is there a specific promise or curiosity hook?
4. Does the email deliver on the subject line's promise?
5. Would this look out of place next to emails from friends?
