# Offer-Audience Matching Rubric

## Purpose
Evaluate if the offer and value exchange match the audience's readiness and situation.

## Input Required
This rubric requires audience parameters:
- **Awareness Level:** Where are they in the awareness spectrum?
- **Traffic Temperature:** Cold, Warm, or Hot?
- **Problem Urgency:** How acute is their need?
- **Budget Level:** What price range is appropriate?

## Value Exchange Evaluation

### The Core Equation
Perceived Value Received > Perceived Cost Given

Cost includes:
- Money
- Email (privacy)
- Time
- Effort
- Risk

### Offer Types by Readiness

| Readiness | Appropriate Offers |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Very Cold | Free content, no email required |
| Cold | Lead magnet for email |
| Warming | Free trial, demo, consultation |
| Warm | Low-risk purchase, starter tier |
| Hot | Full purchase, premium tier |

## Matching Requirements

### Lead Magnet Offers
**For cold/warming audiences**

**Required elements:**
- Specific, immediately valuable content
- Solves a small but real problem
- Quick to consume (not overwhelming)
- Clear preview of what's inside
- Low ask (just email, maybe name)

**Red Flags:**
- "Newsletter" as the value (too vague)
- Asking for phone number/company size
- Content too long or overwhelming
- Unclear what they'll receive

### Free Trial/Demo Offers
**For warming/warm audiences**

**Required elements:**
- Clear what the trial includes
- Easy onboarding (no credit card ideal)
- Time to actually experience value
- Support during trial
- Clear path to conversion

**Red Flags:**
- Credit card required (for cold traffic)
- Trial too short to experience value
- Complicated setup that creates friction
- No guidance on what to do

### Purchase Offers
**For warm/hot audiences**

**Required elements:**
- Clear pricing with no surprises
- Strong guarantee matching risk
- Multiple payment options
- Clear what's included
- Easy purchase path

**Red Flags:**
- Hidden costs
- Weak guarantee
- Complicated checkout
- Unclear deliverables

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Ask-Readiness Match (Weight: 35%)
- Is commitment level appropriate for awareness?
- Is the ask proportional to established trust?
- Would THIS visitor be comfortable with THIS ask?

### 2. Value Clarity (Weight: 25%)
- Is what they'll receive crystal clear?
- Are deliverables specific, not vague?
- Can they visualize the value?

### 3. Value-Cost Ratio (Weight: 25%)
- Does perceived value exceed perceived cost?
- Is the exchange obviously favorable to them?
- Would a skeptic see the value?

### 4. Friction Level (Weight: 15%)
- Is the process easy for the commitment level?
- Are unnecessary fields removed?
- Is the path to value clear?

## Common Mismatches

| Mismatch | Problem |
|----------|---------|
| Purchase ask to cold traffic | Too much too soon |
| Newsletter to hot traffic | Underselling the moment |
| Complex form for lead magnet | Friction exceeds value |
| Simple download for warm traffic | Missing the sale |

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10  | Perfect match between offer, ask, and audience readiness |
| 7-8   | Good match with minor friction or value-ask gaps |
| 5-6   | Reasonable but notable misalignment |
| 3-4   | Clear mismatch in ask level or value communication |
| 1-2   | Complete mismatch (purchase to cold, newsletter to hot) |
