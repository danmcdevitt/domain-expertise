# Awareness Level Matching Rubric

## Purpose
Evaluate if landing page messaging matches the visitor's awareness level.

## Input Required
This rubric requires the visitor's awareness level as input:
- **Unaware** - Doesn't know they have a problem
- **Problem Aware** - Feels pain but doesn't know solutions exist
- **Solution Aware** - Knows solutions exist but not your brand
- **Product Aware** - Knows your brand but not convinced
- **Most Aware** - Ready to buy, needs the offer

## Matching Requirements by Level

### Unaware Visitors
**Required elements:**
- Educational content explaining the problem exists
- Relatable scenarios that create recognition
- "You might be experiencing..." framing
- Longer copy that builds awareness

**Red Flags:**
- Jumping straight to product features
- Assuming visitor knows terminology
- Direct sales pitch
- Short-form content

### Problem Aware Visitors
**Required elements:**
- Problem agitation (consequences of not solving)
- Solution category introduction
- "There's a better way" framing
- Bridge from pain to solution

**Red Flags:**
- No problem acknowledgment
- Straight to product comparison
- Assumption they know about solutions

### Solution Aware Visitors
**Required elements:**
- Clear differentiation from alternatives
- Why your approach is better
- Feature/benefit comparison
- Unique mechanism or methodology

**Red Flags:**
- Generic "we're the best" claims
- No differentiation from competition
- Problem-heavy content they've moved past

### Product Aware Visitors
**Required elements:**
- Social proof and testimonials
- Objection handling
- Detailed feature information
- Case studies and results

**Red Flags:**
- Re-explaining basics they know
- Not addressing specific objections
- Missing proof of claims

### Most Aware Visitors
**Required elements:**
- Clear, prominent offer
- High-urgency CTA
- Price and terms upfront
- Fast path to purchase

**Red Flags:**
- Too much educational content
- Buried CTA
- No urgency
- Excessive copy before offer

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Copy Length Match (Weight: 30%)
- Does copy length match awareness level?
- Unaware = long form, Most Aware = short form
- Is there appropriate depth for their knowledge level?

### 2. Starting Point Match (Weight: 30%)
- Does the copy start where they are mentally?
- Does it assume appropriate prior knowledge?
- Is the entry point neither too basic nor too advanced?

### 3. Content Type Match (Weight: 25%)
- Unaware needs education, Most Aware needs offer
- Is the content type appropriate?
- Is emphasis on right element for their stage?

### 4. Ask Appropriateness (Weight: 15%)
- Is the CTA appropriate for their awareness level?
- Is commitment ask proportional to awareness?

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10  | Perfect match between awareness level and messaging |
| 7-8   | Good match with minor misfits |
| 5-6   | Somewhat matched but notable gaps |
| 3-4   | Significant mismatch in approach |
| 1-2   | Complete mismatch (e.g., sales pitch to unaware) |
